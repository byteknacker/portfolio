John's Portfolio Website

My name is Xiangyu John Wu, and I have been programming ever since I began my startup Career Harbour Ltd.

I built this portfolio site for job applications. However, I also hope that it may inspire those who are new to programming to dive into an exciting world of endless possibilities.

My interest lies in SMEs, FinTech and Artificial Intelligence.

I specialise in creating dynamic web applications using Python, Django, and the LAMP stack. My DevOps are self-created using Vagrant and other HashiCorp technologies. I recently aggregated my knowledge and skills in DevOps into DevBlend, an Open Source organisation dedicated to create elegant and simple automated DevOps tools for developers.

Why I Program

When I started out, it was more like a necessity. Coders are expensive to hire in London, UK, and also hard to manage. I resolved to learn to code myself during the last year of my degree at University College London. I began with video tutorials but then quickly switched to more sophisticated textbooks and official documentations.

After a few weeks, I discovered that programming was my passion which I was searching for a long time. After listening to Steve Job's Stanford commencement speech, I am sure to have discovered the love of my life and will continue with it in the future.

The reason why programming is attractive to me is that you can create out of thin air something that is extremely useful. It abridges labour. For example, an app that automatically calls people and connects to you only when they pick up. It is the process of creating something useful that drives my passion for programming.

How I Program

SCRUM

For web apps developments, SCRUM is highly efficient and yields tangible results within a very short time, often in just a few weeks. I learned SCRUM management and practiced it for over a year at Career Harbour with a small but agile team of developers, designers, and business developers. We created numours prototypes of custom web apps such as a reporting dashboard on company data for job seekers.

Multi-Tech

I use a combination of programming languages, frameworks, and custom-created tools to create web apps that can power dynamic and data driven use cases. Due to my experienced skills in DevOps, I am able to quickly deploy apps within a Continuos Integration system. Development, testing, and deployment are done automatically and with one-click. My focus lies on integrating multiple technologies into a functional whole.

Routine

Coding needs to be cultivated. Hence I developed daily routines to enforce a healthy habit. The todo list everyday includes: contributing to a GitHub Open Source project, build my own apps that I use myself, study programming textbooks, help at least one person in the FCC community on a coding problem. This keeps me busy with coding and practicing my existing skills and I will not get rusty when I get on with a new job.

Up-to-date

I am open to new technologies and watch the news on TechCrunch, Bloomberg, Wired and Entrepreneur on a daily basis. Whenever I get to know a new technology that I find to be useful, I start learning it immediately and implement in one of my existing projects. I also attend regularly programmer's meetups such as the FCC Vienna Meetup. This helps to keep myself up-to-date with current technology trends.
